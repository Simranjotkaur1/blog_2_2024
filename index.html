Women can exercise less often than men and still see greater health benefits, new study shows

A recent study found that women may gain more health benefits from exercise than men, even with less time spent on physical activity. Analyzing data from over 400,000 adults, researchers observed that women who did 2.5 hours of moderate exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous exercise weekly had a 24% lower risk of death, compared to a 15% reduction for men doing the same. Men required about five hours of weekly exercise for a similar mortality benefit. While reasons for this difference remain unclear, anatomical factors may play a role. Despite study limitations, experts agree that regular exercise is essential for heart health and recommend aerobic and strength-training activities that are enjoyable and sustainable.
